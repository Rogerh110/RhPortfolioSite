Challenge & Constraints

This is a design project to design a trademark and cohesive identity system for an organization. The project comprises three phases: organizational research, trademark design, and trademark application.

The organizational research phase of the process is to identify the brand platform of the organization that consists of the personality, the targeted audience, and the key competitors of the organization. The design of the trademark and the graphic elements should convey the personality and the essence of the organization clearly and equip the organization to broaden and utilize the visual identity in trademark applications.

Solution

The existing identity does not comprise a systematic use of color and graphic elements, and the logo for the organization is outdated and generic. Therefore, the main goal was to redesign the trademark and establish a unique visual identity system that helps the organization stand out among the competitors.

The redesigned trademark comprises a logotype of the Friends of the Parks and a logomark. The logomark consists of five-leaf shapes that derive from the eye of the letter e in the logotype. It indicates that the Friends of the Parks are inclusive to all communities and conversations between activists and professionals for the local environmental matters. The concept of parks being natural, healthy, and growing embeds in the green color and the tree in the negative space.
