About

Dodd Camera is a store that carries brand-name cameras & lenses, plus a large selection of gear for photography. Dodd Camera was founded in 1891 in Cleveland, Ohio. It was founded as an architecture supply store, stocking a variety of drawing and art supplies. By the early 1900s, Dodd Camera partnered with Eastman Kodak to bring his revolutionary camera to the market. They operate 8 local camera stores throughout Ohio and opened their first Chicago store in 2008. Each of these stores provides excellent customer service, friendly and knowledgeable sales staff, as well as classes and events throughout the year.

Challenge & Constraints

The redesigned trademark must be strictly a typographic solution; letterforms can be redrawn, modified, or invented with no additional symbol introduced as an independent or significant element. The redesign must be a clear improvement: e.g., by way of simplification, modernization, legibility, sophistication, etc.

Solution

To carry the legacy of Dodd company as a business that has a long history and to celebrate the partnership with Kodak company, the redesigned trademark is formed of four closed letterforms that share homogeneous oval shapes. The decline in the height of letters applied in the trademark derived from the reminiscent camera bellows that many Kodak camera models had as a visual element to the trademark itself.
